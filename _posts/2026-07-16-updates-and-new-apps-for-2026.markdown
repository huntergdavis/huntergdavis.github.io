## PS1 Web Player

While working on a new Playstation (1) game about my beagle, I realized that web emulation of PS1 games is a solved problem, and I shouldn't be making folks use an emulater when a modern web browser will do.  I've added this to the Johnny Castaway PS1 project as well, so you can play that port and this game in your brower (tested on Android mobile as well)

- [Johnny Castaway PS1 web emulator](https://hunterdavis.com/johnny-castaway-ps1/play/online/)
- [Zoomies PS1 web emulator](https://hunterdavis.com/zoomies/play/)

## Zoomies PS1

I've been working on a fun-focused PS1 game, an infinite runner. It's based on my real beagle, Munchie (Munchii in the game), it's called Zoomies. It's coming along nicely, with a fun rez-like mechanic where your health dictates the graphical fidelity of the game. It's kind of the example game for a PS1 game engine I've been having fun building. It's kind of just a silly game.

## Dunking Sheep

Remember dunking bird?  I had made it multi-dunk able, but at the end of the day it no longer fit my workflow.  I love me some [herdr](https://herdr.dev). So I've re-created dunking bird to work directly via herdr's socket interface and it's a lot more direct.  Now you can auto-dunk on all of your agents across all of your herder panes.  Don't trust your agent's auto-time ability?  Want a centralised way to push all your various AI agents through to real gains without having to use yet another AI to orchestrate?  Dunk away!
